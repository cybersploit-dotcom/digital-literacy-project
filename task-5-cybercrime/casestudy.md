# Cybercrime Case Study — Phishing

## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers disguise themselves as trusted 
entities such as banks, colleges, or government portals to trick victims 
into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or bank details.

## How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. Victim receives an email or SMS that looks official
   (e.g., "Your SBI account will be blocked — verify now")
2. A fake link is included that leads to a fraudulent website
   that looks identical to the real one
3. Victim enters their login credentials or OTP
4. Attacker captures this data and gains unauthorized access
5. Victim's bank account or personal data is compromised

## Who is Targeted?
Students, senior citizens, and first-time internet users are most 
commonly targeted due to lower awareness about cybercrime tactics.

## Consequences
- Financial loss through unauthorized transactions
- Identity theft and misuse of personal information
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital platforms
- In India, thousands of UPI fraud cases are linked to phishing every year

Source: cybercrime.gov.in, CERT-In Annual Report
